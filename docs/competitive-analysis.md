> **Primary source:** [aidocs/frantelligence-prd.md §4](../aidocs/frantelligence-prd.md#4-competitive-landscape) (tables: FranConnect + Frannie AI, Franchise Systems AI, Harmonyize, adjacent tools).

---

## Summary from PRD

- **Direct competitors** combine franchise ops suites **or** AI-first ops narratives; Frantelligence differentiates on **multi-channel delivery** (Slack / Teams / SMS), **RAG on brand docs**, **financial coaching**, and **mid-market** self-serve motion.
- **Adjacent** tools (Trainual, Guru/Glean, etc.) validate demand for knowledge + AI but lack franchise tenancy and combined ops stack.

---

## What building Knowledge Builder added (after midterm)

From **feature plan** and **external research** ([gapAutoDoc_perplexity](../ai/guides/external/gapAutoDoc_perplexity.md)):

- **Gap → draft → review → publish** is **table stakes** in enterprise knowledge / support (Zendesk, Glean, etc.) — **not** unique in the abstract; uniqueness is **franchise-specific** workflow, **multi-tenant** isolation, and **field** context (KI + KB + ops).
- **Sub-plan 4** (rich context pipeline) explicitly closes the quality gap vs **generic** drafts by tying generation to **existing KB chunks, tickets, and public team chat** — a **build-learned** response to “plausible but wrong” generic drafts.

## Positioning consequence

- **Sales motion** still leads with **KI Chat + KB + channels** (see [mvp.md](./mvp.md) §4 — Knowledge Gaps / Builder “out” of *initial pitch* but **in** for course deliverable).
- **Product strategy:** land with wedge; expand with **usage-in → content-out** flywheel so support deflection **compounds** over time.
